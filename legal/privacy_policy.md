# RiteDoc Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy outlines how ReadyCompliant ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and protects your personal information when you use the RiteDoc software application. This policy is designed to comply with the Privacy Act 1988 (Australia) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

## 1. Information We Collect

ReadyCompliant operates on a principle of absolute data minimisation. The RiteDoc software is designed to process National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) progress notes entirely offline on your local machine.

We collect only the minimal information necessary to provide the service, manage your subscription, and provide technical support.

### 1.1 What We Collect

When you subscribe to and use RiteDoc, we collect the following information:

*   **Contact Information:** Your name, email address, phone number, and business name.
*   **Licence Information:** Your unique licence key, subscription status, and the number of active seats.
*   **Hardware Telemetry:** Basic hardware specifications (RAM, CPU, GPU presence) to determine whether the software runs in Standard or Turbo mode.
*   **Support Data:** Information you provide when submitting a support ticket, including diagnostic logs generated by the self-fix system or Dify bot.
*   **Crash Reports:** Anonymous crash data captured by Sentry to help us identify and fix software bugs.

### 1.2 What We Do NOT Collect

ReadyCompliant explicitly does not collect, transmit, or store any of the following:

*   **Participant Data:** Names, addresses, phone numbers, NDIS numbers, or any personally identifiable information (PII) of NDIS participants.
*   **Progress Notes:** The raw content of support worker progress notes, the processed drafts, or any associated metadata.
*   **Compliance Scores:** The internal 5-pillar compliance scores generated by the local compliance engine.

All processing of progress notes occurs locally on your device. The PII scrubber and the compliance engine operate entirely offline.

## 2. How We Use Your Information

We use the minimal information we collect for the following purposes:

*   **Service Provision:** To validate your licence key, manage your subscription, and ensure the software operates correctly on your hardware.
*   **Billing and Payments:** To process your monthly subscription fees via our third-party payment processor.
*   **Customer Support:** To respond to your inquiries, troubleshoot technical issues, and improve the automated support flow.
*   **Communication:** To send you important updates about the software, cartridge updates, and changes to our terms or policies.
*   **Software Improvement:** To analyse anonymous crash reports and hardware telemetry to optimise the performance and stability of RiteDoc.

## 3. Data Storage and Security

The personal information we collect (contact details, licence keys, support tickets) is stored securely in our Supabase database. We implement industry-standard security measures to protect this data from unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction.

The progress notes and participant data processed by RiteDoc are never stored on our servers. They remain exclusively on your local machine and are subject to a strict 48-hour local auto-delete policy enforced by the software.

## 4. Third-Party Service Providers

We engage a limited number of trusted third-party service providers to facilitate our operations. These providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and use it only for the purposes we specify.

*   **Stripe:** We use Stripe for secure payment processing and subscription management. Stripe collects and processes your payment information in accordance with their own privacy policy.
*   **Brevo:** We use Brevo for transactional emails, such as licence key delivery, password resets, and important service announcements.
*   **Supabase:** We use Supabase as our primary database for managing user accounts, licence keys, and support tickets.
*   **Sentry:** We use Sentry for silent crash reporting. Sentry is configured to capture only technical error data and explicitly excludes any participant PII or progress note content.
*   **Retell & Twilio:** We use Retell AI and Twilio to provide our 24/7 automated phone support. Call transcripts and diagnostic data are stored securely to assist with issue resolution.

## 5. Rights of Individuals

Under the Privacy Act 1988, you have the right to access the personal information we hold about you and to request corrections if you believe the information is inaccurate, incomplete, or out of date.

To request access to or correction of your personal information, please contact us using the details provided below. We will respond to your request within a reasonable timeframe.

## 6. Complaint Process

If you believe that ReadyCompliant has breached the Australian Privacy Principles or mishandled your personal information, you may lodge a formal complaint.

Please submit your complaint in writing to our Privacy Officer. We will acknowledge receipt of your complaint within five (5) business days and conduct a thorough investigation. We aim to resolve all complaints within thirty (30) days.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to escalate your complaint to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).
