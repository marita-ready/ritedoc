# RiteDoc Consolidated Loom Video Guide

This guide outlines the structure and script talking points for a consolidated series of 2-3 short Loom videos designed to replace the previous 21-episode series. These videos will serve as the primary visual onboarding and training material for new RiteDoc clients.

## Video 1: Getting Started & The "Why" (2-3 Minutes)

**Goal:** Welcome the client, explain the core value proposition (offline, secure, compliance-focused), and show them how to install and activate the software.

### What to Show (Screen Recording)

1.  **Start:** The ReadyCompliant welcome email (blurring any actual licence keys).
2.  **Action:** Clicking the download link and running the RiteDoc installer (Windows/Mac).
3.  **Action:** Opening the app for the first time and pasting the licence key into the activation screen.
4.  **Visual:** The main RiteDoc dashboard appearing after successful activation.

### Script Talking Points

*   **Welcome:** "Hi, I'm Marita from ReadyCompliant. Welcome to RiteDoc. We built this tool because we know how much time admin teams spend cleaning up support worker notes before audits."
*   **The "Why":** "RiteDoc is different. It's not a cloud app. It runs entirely offline on your computer. This means your participant data *never* leaves your machine. It's the most secure way to process NDIS progress notes."
*   **Installation:** "Getting started is easy. Just download the installer from your welcome email, run it, and paste in your unique licence key."
*   **Activation:** "Once activated, RiteDoc will do a quick check of your computer's hardware to make sure it runs as fast as possible. Then, you're ready to go."

## Video 2: The Daily Workflow (Import, Process, Export) (4-5 Minutes)

**Goal:** Demonstrate the core functionality of RiteDoc using a sample CSV file. Show the PII scrubbing, the traffic light system, and how to handle flagged notes.

### What to Show (Screen Recording)

1.  **Start:** The main RiteDoc dashboard.
2.  **Action:** Clicking "Import CSV" and selecting a sample file (ensure the sample file contains *fake* data, e.g., `synthetic_dirty_notes.csv`).
3.  **Action:** Clicking "Process Notes". Show the progress bar as the local AI engine works.
4.  **Visual:** The results screen displaying the processed drafts and the Traffic Light indicators (GREEN, ORANGE, RED).
5.  **Action:** Highlighting a GREEN note (audit-ready).
6.  **Action:** Highlighting an ORANGE note. Point out a bracketed flag (e.g., `[MISSING OUTCOME]`) and demonstrate manually typing in the missing information.
7.  **Action:** Highlighting a RED note (incident detected). Explain why it floated to the top.
8.  **Action:** Clicking "Export Processed CSV" and saving the file.

### Script Talking Points

*   **Importing:** "Here's how you'll use RiteDoc every day. First, export your raw notes from your CRM—like ShiftCare or Brevity—as a CSV file. Then, just click 'Import' in RiteDoc."
*   **Processing (The Magic):** "When you click 'Process', our local compliance engine goes to work. First, it scrubs all the PII—names, addresses, phone numbers—replacing them with tags. Then, it rewrites the notes into professional, audit-ready drafts based on your state's specific NDIS rules."
*   **Traffic Lights:** "When it's done, you'll see our Traffic Light system. GREEN means the note is perfect. ORANGE means it needs a little more detail—just look for the bracketed text and fill in the blanks. RED means the engine detected a potential incident or severe risk, so those always float to the top for your immediate review."
*   **Exporting:** "Once you're happy, click 'Export'. You now have a clean CSV file ready to import back into your CRM. And remember, RiteDoc automatically deletes all this data from your computer after 48 hours for total security."

## Video 3: Help & Support (1-2 Minutes)

**Goal:** Show clients how to use the automated support stack if they run into issues, emphasising that help is always available.

### What to Show (Screen Recording)

1.  **Start:** The main RiteDoc dashboard.
2.  **Action:** Clicking the "Help" or "Support" icon to open the Dify bot interface.
3.  **Action:** Typing a sample question into the bot (e.g., "My CSV won't import").
4.  **Visual:** The bot providing a step-by-step troubleshooting response.
5.  **Visual:** A slide or graphic displaying the 24/7 Retell AI phone support number.

### Script Talking Points

*   **Self-Fix:** "We've built RiteDoc to be as reliable as possible. In fact, if it detects a minor issue—like running low on memory—it will often fix itself silently in the background."
*   **In-App Bot:** "But if you do get stuck, help is built right in. Just click the Help icon to chat with our support assistant. It can guide you through most common problems, like formatting your CSV files correctly."
*   **Phone Support:** "And if you need more help, we have a 24/7 automated phone line. Just call the number on the screen, and our AI agent will walk you through the solution or escalate your ticket to our human team if needed. We're always here to help you."
