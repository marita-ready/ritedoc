# RiteDoc FAQ

Welcome to the RiteDoc Frequently Asked Questions. Here you will find answers to common questions about our offline Windows desktop app designed for NDIS provider admin teams.

## Product

**What is RiteDoc?**
RiteDoc is an offline Windows desktop application (built on Tauri 2.0) that rewrites raw NDIS support worker progress notes into audit-prepared drafts. It uses a local AI model (Phi-4-mini) to process notes securely on your machine.

**Who is it for?**
RiteDoc is designed specifically for NDIS provider admin teams, not for individual support workers.

**Does it work on Mac?**
Currently, RiteDoc is available for Windows only at launch.

**What are the minimum computer specs?**
To run RiteDoc smoothly, your computer should have at least 16GB of RAM, an Intel i7 or i5 processor, and run Windows 10 or 11.

**Does it need internet?**
No, RiteDoc is fully offline. No data ever leaves your computer, ensuring complete privacy and security.

**What platforms does it work with?**
RiteDoc works with any platform that supports CSV export, including ShiftCare, Brevity, SupportAbility, Lumary, CareMaster, and Astalty.

**How long does it take to process a batch?**
Processing times vary based on your hardware. On minimum specs, it typically takes 5 to 13 minutes to process a batch of 50 notes. It will be faster on better hardware.

**What does the traffic light system mean?**
RiteDoc uses a traffic light system to indicate the status of each note:
- **Green:** Good to go.
- **Orange:** Missing data (includes prompts for what is needed).
- **Red:** An incident occurred, and the required documentation is pre-filled.

## Privacy & Security

**Where does my data go?**
Nowhere. All data stays entirely on your computer.

**Is it cloud-based?**
No, RiteDoc is fully offline.

**Does it store my notes?**
Notes are only stored locally on your machine, and you have full control over their deletion.

## Workflow

**How do I get my notes into RiteDoc?**
Simply export a CSV file from your platform and import it into RiteDoc.

**How do I get the rewritten notes back into my platform?**
You can copy each note using the one-click copy button and paste it back into your platform's note field. RiteDoc features a platform-aware clipboard that matches your system's fields.

**Can I bulk import notes back into my platform?**
No. Most NDIS platforms do not accept CSV imports for progress notes. The process requires copying and pasting, but we have made it as fast as possible with per-field copy buttons.

**Do I need to review the drafts?**
**YES.** RiteDoc produces audit-prepared drafts, not final documentation. Every draft must be reviewed and approved by your team before being entered into your platform.
