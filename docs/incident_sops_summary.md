# NDIS Incident Response: Category-Specific Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

This document outlines the mandatory procedural steps, legislative sources, and reporting requirements for the 14 red flag categories used in the RiteDoc compliance engine.

## 1. Unauthorised Restrictive Practice

**Legislative Source:** NDIS (Restrictive Practices and Behaviour Support) Rules 2018; NDIS (Incident Management and Reportable Incidents) Rules 2018

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ensure immediate safety of the participant and others | `made safe`, `checked for injuries`, `separated`, `removed from danger`, `calmed down`, `de-escalated` |
| 2 | Cease the unauthorised restrictive practice as soon as it is safe to do so | `stopped`, `released`, `let go`, `unlocked`, `removed restraint`, `ceased practice` |
| 3 | Notify supervisor or manager immediately | `called manager`, `notified supervisor`, `told coordinator`, `rang on-call`, `reported to team leader` |
| 4 | Document the incident in the internal Incident Register | `filled out incident report`, `logged in system`, `wrote incident form`, `completed IR`, `documented in register` |
| 5 | Notify the NDIS Commission via the My Reportable Incidents portal within 5 business days (or 24 hours if serious harm occurred) | `NDIS portal`, `reportable incident form`, `notified commission`, `submitted to NDIS`, `5 day form`, `24 hour notification` |
| 6 | Consult with a specialist behaviour support practitioner to review or develop a Behaviour Support Plan | `contacted BSP`, `referred to practitioner`, `requested behaviour assessment`, `BSP review`, `behaviour specialist` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** 5 business days (24 hours if serious harm)
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, Authorised Program Officer (APO)
- **External Notifications:** NDIS Commission, Behaviour Support Practitioner, Family/Guardian
- **Required Documentation:** Incident Report, Notice of a Reportable Incident Form, Behaviour Support Plan (BSP)

### Victorian-Specific Requirements

- **Notify the Victorian Senior Practitioner via the Restrictive Intervention Data System (RIDS) within 1 business day for emergency use:** Emergency use of a restrictive practice must be reported to the Senior Practitioner via RIDS within 1 business day.
- **Authorised Program Officer (APO) must review the use of the restrictive practice:** The APO must review the incident to ensure it was the least restrictive option and refer for reassessment.

## 2. Medication Error / Missed Medication

**Legislative Source:** NDIS Practice Standards - Core Module 4 (Provision of Supports Environment); NDIS (Incident Management and Reportable Incidents) Rules 2018

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Acknowledge the error and stay calm | `realised mistake`, `noticed error`, `found missed dose`, `identified wrong med` |
| 2 | Check the participant's vital signs and monitor for adverse reactions | `checked vitals`, `monitored`, `observed for side effects`, `checked breathing`, `took temperature`, `asked how they feel` |
| 3 | Contact a medical professional (doctor, pharmacist, or 000 if emergency) for advice | `called doctor`, `rang GP`, `spoke to pharmacist`, `called 000`, `called ambulance`, `sought medical advice`, `called healthdirect` |
| 4 | Follow the medical advice provided | `followed doctor's orders`, `did what pharmacist said`, `administered as advised`, `took to hospital` |
| 5 | Notify supervisor or manager immediately | `called manager`, `notified supervisor`, `told coordinator`, `rang on-call` |
| 6 | Document the error on the Medication Administration Record (MAR) chart and complete an internal incident report | `updated MAR`, `signed chart`, `wrote on medication sheet`, `filled out incident report`, `logged error` |
| 7 | If the error caused serious harm, notify the NDIS Commission within 24 hours | `NDIS portal`, `reportable incident`, `24 hour notification`, `notified commission` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting immediately; NDIS Commission within 24 hours if serious harm
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager
- **External Notifications:** Medical Professional (GP/Pharmacist/000), Family/Guardian, NDIS Commission (if harm)
- **Required Documentation:** Incident Report, Medication Administration Record (MAR), Medical Notes

## 3. Injury / Fall / Medical Emergency

**Legislative Source:** NDIS (Incident Management and Reportable Incidents) Rules 2018; Work Health and Safety Act 2011

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Provide immediate first aid or call 000 for emergency medical assistance | `first aid`, `CPR`, `called 000`, `called ambulance`, `applied bandage`, `stopped bleeding`, `paramedics` |
| 2 | Ensure the environment is safe to prevent further injury | `cleared area`, `removed hazard`, `made safe`, `cleaned up spill`, `blocked off area` |
| 3 | Notify supervisor or manager immediately | `called manager`, `notified supervisor`, `told coordinator`, `rang on-call` |
| 4 | Notify family, guardian, or emergency contact | `called mum`, `notified family`, `rang next of kin`, `contacted guardian` |
| 5 | Document the incident in the internal Incident Register | `filled out incident report`, `logged in system`, `wrote incident form`, `completed IR` |
| 6 | If it is a serious injury, notify the NDIS Commission within 24 hours | `NDIS portal`, `reportable incident`, `24 hour notification`, `notified commission` |
| 7 | If it meets the definition of a notifiable incident under WHS Act, notify the state WHS regulator immediately and preserve the site | `notified WorkSafe`, `called SafeWork`, `preserved site`, `WHS notification` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** 24 hours for NDIS Commission (if serious); Immediately for WHS regulator (if notifiable)
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, WHS Officer
- **External Notifications:** Emergency Services (000), Family/Guardian, NDIS Commission, State WHS Regulator
- **Required Documentation:** Incident Report, First Aid Record, Notice of a Reportable Incident Form, WHS Notification Form

### Victorian-Specific Requirements

- **Notify WorkSafe Victoria immediately if it is a notifiable incident:** Under the OHS Act 2004 (Vic), WorkSafe must be notified immediately of incidents resulting in death or serious injury.

## 4. Abuse / Neglect / Exploitation Indicators

**Legislative Source:** NDIS (Incident Management and Reportable Incidents) Rules 2018; NDIS Code of Conduct

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ensure the immediate safety of the participant and separate them from the alleged perpetrator | `made safe`, `separated`, `removed from situation`, `took to safe place`, `protected` |
| 2 | Provide immediate medical or psychological support if required | `first aid`, `called doctor`, `comforted`, `offered support`, `called counselor` |
| 3 | Contact Police (000) if a crime is suspected or there is immediate danger | `called police`, `rang 000`, `reported to police`, `police attended` |
| 4 | Notify supervisor or manager immediately | `called manager`, `notified supervisor`, `told coordinator`, `rang on-call` |
| 5 | Preserve any evidence related to the alleged abuse or neglect | `kept evidence`, `didn't wash clothes`, `took photos`, `secured area`, `saved messages` |
| 6 | Notify the NDIS Commission via the My Reportable Incidents portal within 24 hours | `NDIS portal`, `reportable incident`, `24 hour notification`, `notified commission` |
| 7 | Provide the participant with information about access to independent advocates | `offered advocate`, `gave advocacy info`, `contacted advocate`, `explained rights` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** 24 hours
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, Management Team
- **External Notifications:** Police (if crime), NDIS Commission, Family/Guardian (unless implicated), Independent Advocate
- **Required Documentation:** Incident Report, Notice of a Reportable Incident Form, Police Report Number

## 5. Missing Consent / Capacity Concerns

**Legislative Source:** NDIS Practice Standards - Core Module 1 (Rights and Responsibilities); NDIS Code of Conduct

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pause the support or intervention if consent is unclear or withdrawn | `stopped`, `paused`, `waited`, `did not proceed`, `held off` |
| 2 | Explain the support/intervention using the participant's preferred language and mode of communication | `explained`, `used easy read`, `used pictures`, `talked through it`, `used communication device` |
| 3 | Allow the participant sufficient time to consider their options and seek advice | `gave time`, `let them think`, `came back later`, `didn't rush` |
| 4 | If capacity is still a concern, involve the participant's nominated decision-maker, guardian, or advocate | `called guardian`, `spoke to nominee`, `contacted advocate`, `involved family` |
| 5 | Document the consent process, the participant's response, and any decisions made in the progress notes | `documented consent`, `wrote in notes`, `recorded decision`, `noted refusal` |
| 6 | Notify supervisor if the lack of consent prevents essential care or poses a risk | `notified supervisor`, `called manager`, `reported refusal of care` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting via progress notes; escalate immediately if risk of harm
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager
- **External Notifications:** Guardian/Nominee, Advocate
- **Required Documentation:** Progress Notes, Consent Form (if applicable)

### Victorian-Specific Requirements

- **Consult the Office of the Public Advocate (OPA) if a guardian needs to be appointed:** If the participant lacks capacity for a significant decision and has no guardian, an application to VCAT may be required.

## 6. Property Damage / Financial Irregularity

**Legislative Source:** NDIS Practice Standards - Core Module 4 (Participant Money and Property); Australian Consumer Law

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ensure the immediate safety of the participant and others if property damage involves a hazard | `made safe`, `cleaned up glass`, `removed hazard`, `secured area` |
| 2 | Document the damage or financial discrepancy immediately with clear details (and photos if applicable) | `took photos`, `counted money`, `checked receipts`, `noted damage`, `recorded missing funds` |
| 3 | Notify supervisor or manager immediately | `called manager`, `notified supervisor`, `reported missing money`, `told coordinator` |
| 4 | Complete an internal incident report detailing the property damage or financial irregularity | `filled out incident report`, `logged in system`, `wrote incident form` |
| 5 | If theft or fraud is suspected, contact the Police | `called police`, `reported theft`, `police report` |
| 6 | If the incident involves financial abuse or exploitation by a worker, notify the NDIS Commission within 24 hours | `NDIS portal`, `reportable incident`, `24 hour notification`, `reported financial abuse` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting immediately; 24 hours for NDIS Commission if financial abuse
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, Finance Team
- **External Notifications:** Police (if theft/fraud), NDIS Commission (if abuse), Family/Guardian
- **Required Documentation:** Incident Report, Financial Records/Receipts, Photographs of Damage

## 7. Behavioural Incident Requiring Reporting

**Legislative Source:** NDIS (Restrictive Practices and Behaviour Support) Rules 2018; NDIS Practice Standards

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Implement proactive and reactive strategies as outlined in the participant's Behaviour Support Plan (BSP) | `followed BSP`, `used proactive strategies`, `implemented reactive strategies`, `de-escalated`, `redirected` |
| 2 | Ensure the safety of the participant, staff, and others in the environment | `made safe`, `moved others away`, `cleared area`, `maintained safe distance` |
| 3 | If the behaviour results in serious injury or involves an unauthorised restrictive practice, notify supervisor immediately | `called manager`, `notified supervisor`, `reported injury`, `reported restraint` |
| 4 | Document the behaviour, triggers, and strategies used in the progress notes and/or behaviour tracking sheet | `recorded ABC data`, `filled out behaviour chart`, `wrote in progress notes`, `documented triggers` |
| 5 | Complete an internal incident report if the behaviour caused harm, property damage, or required emergency intervention | `filled out incident report`, `logged in system`, `completed IR` |
| 6 | Notify the NDIS Commission if the incident meets the criteria for a reportable incident (e.g., serious injury, unauthorised RP) | `NDIS portal`, `reportable incident`, `notified commission` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting by end of shift; NDIS Commission within 24h/5 days if reportable criteria met
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager
- **External Notifications:** Behaviour Support Practitioner, NDIS Commission (if reportable)
- **Required Documentation:** Progress Notes, Behaviour Tracking Data (ABC chart), Incident Report

### Victorian-Specific Requirements

- **Report to Victorian Senior Practitioner via RIDS if a regulated restrictive practice was used:** Must be reported monthly, or within 1 business day if it was an emergency use.

## 8. Worker Safety / WHS Concerns

**Legislative Source:** Work Health and Safety Act 2011; NDIS Practice Standards

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Remove yourself and others from immediate danger | `stepped back`, `left the room`, `evacuated`, `moved to safety` |
| 2 | Seek immediate first aid or medical attention if injured | `first aid`, `went to doctor`, `called ambulance`, `treated injury` |
| 3 | Notify supervisor or manager immediately | `called manager`, `notified supervisor`, `reported to WHS officer` |
| 4 | Complete an internal incident/hazard report detailing the safety concern or injury | `filled out hazard report`, `completed WHS form`, `logged safety incident`, `wrote incident report` |
| 5 | If it is a 'notifiable incident' (death, serious injury/illness, dangerous incident), the PCBU must notify the state WHS regulator immediately | `notified WorkSafe`, `called SafeWork`, `WHS notification` |
| 6 | Preserve the incident site until an inspector arrives or directs otherwise (for notifiable incidents) | `preserved site`, `didn't touch anything`, `cordoned off area` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting immediately; WHS regulator immediately for notifiable incidents
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, WHS Officer/Representative
- **External Notifications:** State WHS Regulator (e.g., SafeWork/WorkSafe)
- **Required Documentation:** WHS Incident/Hazard Report, First Aid Record, Medical Certificate

### Victorian-Specific Requirements

- **Notify WorkSafe Victoria immediately for notifiable incidents:** Under the OHS Act 2004 (Vic), employers must notify WorkSafe immediately after becoming aware of a notifiable incident.

## 9. Environmental / WHS Hazards

**Legislative Source:** Work Health and Safety Act 2011; NDIS Practice Standards - Core Module 4 (Safe Environment)

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify and assess the environmental hazard | `noticed hazard`, `spotted risk`, `identified danger`, `found unsafe area` |
| 2 | Take immediate action to eliminate or minimise the risk (e.g., clean up spill, block access) | `cleaned up`, `blocked off`, `put up sign`, `removed trip hazard`, `fixed issue` |
| 3 | If the hazard cannot be immediately resolved, isolate the area and warn others | `isolated area`, `warned staff`, `told participant`, `locked door` |
| 4 | Notify supervisor or manager about the hazard | `called manager`, `notified supervisor`, `reported hazard` |
| 5 | Complete an internal hazard report form | `filled out hazard report`, `logged in system`, `completed WHS form` |
| 6 | Follow up to ensure the hazard has been permanently resolved | `checked repair`, `confirmed fixed`, `maintenance completed` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting by end of shift or immediately if high risk
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, Maintenance Team, WHS Officer
- **External Notifications:** Landlord/Property Manager (if applicable)
- **Required Documentation:** Hazard Report Form, Maintenance Request

## 10. Participant Rights Violations

**Legislative Source:** NDIS Practice Standards - Core Module 1 (Rights and Responsibilities); NDIS Code of Conduct

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Intervene immediately if you witness a violation of a participant's rights (e.g., loss of dignity, lack of privacy, denied choice) | `stepped in`, `stopped the action`, `spoke up`, `advocated for participant`, `ensured privacy` |
| 2 | Support the participant and acknowledge their feelings and rights | `apologised`, `comforted`, `validated feelings`, `reassured`, `listened` |
| 3 | Notify supervisor or manager immediately | `called manager`, `notified supervisor`, `reported breach`, `told coordinator` |
| 4 | Document the incident objectively in an internal incident report | `filled out incident report`, `logged in system`, `wrote incident form` |
| 5 | Provide the participant with information on how to make a complaint and access an independent advocate | `gave complaints brochure`, `offered advocate`, `explained rights`, `helped make complaint` |
| 6 | If the violation constitutes abuse, neglect, or exploitation, notify the NDIS Commission within 24 hours | `NDIS portal`, `reportable incident`, `24 hour notification`, `reported abuse` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting immediately; 24 hours for NDIS Commission if it constitutes abuse/neglect
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, Quality/Compliance Team
- **External Notifications:** NDIS Commission (if reportable), Independent Advocate
- **Required Documentation:** Incident Report, Complaints Form (if requested)

## 11. Service Delivery Failures

**Legislative Source:** NDIS Practice Standards - Core Module 3 (Provision of Supports); NDIS Code of Conduct

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify the failure (e.g., missed shift, late arrival, incorrect support provided) | `missed shift`, `arrived late`, `couldn't attend`, `wrong support`, `service gap` |
| 2 | Take immediate action to ensure the participant's essential needs (e.g., medication, meals, personal care) are met | `arranged cover`, `stayed late`, `called replacement`, `ensured fed`, `gave meds` |
| 3 | Communicate with the participant (and family/guardian) about the failure and the steps being taken to resolve it | `apologised`, `explained delay`, `called family`, `informed participant` |
| 4 | Notify supervisor or rostering team immediately to arrange alternative support | `called rostering`, `notified supervisor`, `rang on-call`, `requested cover` |
| 5 | Document the service failure and the actions taken in the progress notes and internal incident system | `wrote in notes`, `filled out incident report`, `logged service failure` |
| 6 | If the failure resulted in harm or neglect, notify the NDIS Commission within 24 hours | `NDIS portal`, `reportable incident`, `reported neglect` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting immediately; 24 hours for NDIS Commission if it constitutes neglect
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, Rostering Team
- **External Notifications:** Participant/Family, NDIS Commission (if harm/neglect occurred)
- **Required Documentation:** Progress Notes, Incident Report

## 12. Cultural Safety Concerns

**Legislative Source:** NDIS Practice Standards - Core Module 1 (Individual Values and Beliefs); NDIS Code of Conduct

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Acknowledge the concern and apologise if a culturally unsafe practice occurred | `apologised`, `acknowledged mistake`, `listened to concern`, `respected feedback` |
| 2 | Adjust the support delivery immediately to respect the participant's culture, values, and beliefs | `changed approach`, `adjusted support`, `respected wishes`, `followed cultural protocol` |
| 3 | Discuss the participant's cultural needs and preferences with them to ensure future supports are culturally safe | `asked preferences`, `discussed needs`, `clarified cultural requirements` |
| 4 | Notify supervisor or manager to ensure the participant's support plan is updated with cultural requirements | `notified supervisor`, `requested plan update`, `reported cultural need` |
| 5 | Document the cultural safety concern and the participant's preferences in the progress notes | `wrote in notes`, `documented preference`, `updated profile` |
| 6 | Provide information on how to make a complaint if the participant wishes to do so | `offered complaints process`, `gave feedback form`, `explained how to complain`, `told them about NDIS Commission` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting by end of shift
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager
- **External Notifications:** None (unless escalated to a formal complaint)
- **Required Documentation:** Progress Notes, Updated Support Plan

## 13. Pricing Fraud / Billing Irregularity

**Legislative Source:** NDIS Code of Conduct (Element 8); Australian Consumer Law; NDIS Pricing Arrangements and Price Limits

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify the billing irregularity (e.g., overcharging, charging for unprovided services, wrong price limit) | `noticed overcharge`, `spotted wrong code`, `identified billing error`, `found incorrect invoice` |
| 2 | Do not process or approve the incorrect charge; hold the invoice for review | `held invoice`, `didn't process`, `stopped payment`, `flagged for review` |
| 3 | Notify supervisor, manager, or the finance/billing department immediately | `called manager`, `notified finance`, `reported to billing`, `emailed accounts` |
| 4 | Document the discrepancy clearly, keeping copies of relevant timesheets, invoices, and service agreements | `kept records`, `saved invoice`, `documented error`, `copied timesheet` |
| 5 | Communicate transparently with the participant or plan manager about the error and the steps to rectify it | `informed participant`, `emailed plan manager`, `explained error`, `apologised for billing mistake` |
| 6 | If intentional fraud is suspected, management must report it to the NDIA Fraud Reporting and Investigations team | `reported to NDIA`, `fraud report`, `escalated to compliance` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Internal reporting immediately; NDIA reporting as soon as fraud is suspected
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, Finance/Billing Department, Compliance Officer
- **External Notifications:** Participant/Plan Manager, NDIA Fraud Division (if intentional)
- **Required Documentation:** Invoices, Timesheets, Service Agreement, Internal Incident/Discrepancy Report

## 14. Unresolved Complaint / Grievance

**Legislative Source:** NDIS Practice Standards - Core Module 2B (Complaints Management); NDIS Code of Conduct

### Mandatory Procedural Steps

| Step | Action | Evidence Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Listen to the participant's complaint actively and without defensiveness | `listened`, `heard concern`, `acknowledged complaint`, `took feedback` |
| 2 | Attempt to resolve the issue immediately at the point of service if possible and appropriate | `fixed issue`, `resolved on spot`, `apologised`, `corrected mistake` |
| 3 | If unresolved, provide the participant with the organisation's formal complaints procedure and offer assistance to complete it | `gave complaints form`, `explained process`, `offered help to complain`, `provided brochure` |
| 4 | Inform the participant of their right to access an independent advocate and their right to complain directly to the NDIS Commission | `offered advocate`, `gave NDIS Commission number`, `explained external options` |
| 5 | Notify supervisor or the designated Complaints Officer about the grievance | `notified supervisor`, `reported to complaints officer`, `escalated complaint` |
| 6 | Document the complaint, the participant's desired outcome, and actions taken in the complaints register | `logged in complaints register`, `wrote complaint report`, `documented grievance` |

### Reporting & Notifications

- **Reporting Timeframe:** Acknowledge complaint promptly (usually within 24-48 hours internally)
- **Internal Notifications:** Supervisor/Manager, Complaints Officer
- **External Notifications:** Independent Advocate (if requested)
- **Required Documentation:** Complaints Form, Complaints Register Entry
