# NDIS Incident Documentation Mapping

This document provides a comprehensive guide to incident documentation under the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS), specifically tailored for the RiteDoc compliance engine. It details every type of reportable incident, required documentation, reporting timeframes, restrictive practice requirements, and state-specific rules for Victoria.

## 1. Reportable Incidents Framework

Under the *National Disability Insurance Scheme (Incident Management and Reportable Incidents) Rules 2018*, registered NDIS providers must notify the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission of all reportable incidents that occur, or are alleged to have occurred, in connection with the provision of NDIS supports or services.

### 1.1 Incident Types and Definitions

The NDIS Commission defines six specific categories of reportable incidents:

1.  **Death of a person with disability:** Any death of an NDIS participant, regardless of the cause or location, if it occurs while the participant is receiving supports or services from the provider.
2.  **Serious injury of a person with disability:** An injury that requires medical treatment beyond basic first aid, or results in significant temporary or permanent impairment. This includes fractures, severe burns, deep lacerations, or injuries requiring hospital admission.
3.  **Abuse or neglect of a person with disability:**
    *   **Abuse:** Includes physical, emotional, psychological, financial, or systemic abuse. It encompasses any action that causes harm, distress, or exploitation.
    *   **Neglect:** A failure to provide adequate care, support, or supervision, resulting in harm or a significant risk of harm to the participant's health, safety, or wellbeing. This includes failing to provide necessary medical care, nutrition, or a safe environment.
4.  **Unlawful sexual or physical contact with, or assault of, a person with disability:** Any non-consensual physical or sexual contact, including assault, battery, or any contact that violates the participant's bodily autonomy or legal rights.
5.  **Sexual misconduct committed against, or in the presence of, a person with disability:** This includes grooming, inappropriate sexual comments, exposure to sexual acts, or any sexual behaviour that is non-consensual or inappropriate in the context of service delivery.
6.  **Unauthorised use of a restrictive practice:** The use of any restrictive practice (seclusion, chemical, mechanical, physical, or environmental restraint) that is not authorised by the relevant state or territory body, or is not used in accordance with a current Behaviour Support Plan.

### 1.2 Reporting Timeframes

The NDIS Commission mandates strict timeframes for notifying them of reportable incidents. These timeframes are calculated from the moment the registered NDIS provider becomes aware of the incident.

| Incident Category | Initial Notification Timeframe | Follow-up Report Timeframe | Final Report Timeframe |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Death | 24 hours | 5 business days | 60 days (if requested) |
| Serious Injury | 24 hours | 5 business days | 60 days (if requested) |
| Abuse or Neglect | 24 hours | 5 business days | 60 days (if requested) |
| Unlawful Sexual/Physical Contact | 24 hours | 5 business days | 60 days (if requested) |
| Sexual Misconduct | 24 hours | 5 business days | 60 days (if requested) |
| Unauthorised Restrictive Practice (with harm) | 24 hours | 5 business days | 60 days (if requested) |
| Unauthorised Restrictive Practice (no harm) | 5 business days | N/A (Initial report serves as the 5-day report) | 60 days (if requested) |

### 1.3 Required Forms and Reports

Providers must use the NDIS Commission Portal to submit the required forms.

*   **Immediate Notification Form (24-Hour Form):** Used for all incidents requiring 24-hour notification. It captures essential details about the incident, the participant involved, immediate actions taken to ensure safety, and who has been notified (e.g., police, family).
*   **5-Day Form:** Provides a more detailed account of the incident, including the outcome of any internal investigations, ongoing support provided to the participant, and corrective actions implemented to prevent recurrence. For unauthorised restrictive practices without harm, this is the only form required initially.
*   **Final Report (60-Day Report):** The NDIS Commission may request a final report within 60 days, detailing the comprehensive findings of an internal or external investigation, long-term systemic changes, and the final resolution of the incident.

## 2. Restrictive Practices and Behaviour Support

The NDIS strictly regulates the use of restrictive practices to protect the rights and dignity of participants.

### 2.1 Types of Regulated Restrictive Practices

The *National Disability Insurance Scheme (Restrictive Practices and Behaviour Support) Rules 2018* define five regulated restrictive practices:

1.  **Seclusion:** Sole confinement of a person with disability in a room or a physical space at any hour of the day or night where voluntary exit is prevented, or not facilitated, or it is implied that voluntary exit is not permitted.
2.  **Chemical Restraint:** The use of medication or chemical substance for the primary purpose of influencing a person's behaviour. It does not include the use of medication prescribed by a medical practitioner for the treatment of, or to enable treatment of, a diagnosed mental disorder, a physical illness, or a physical condition.
3.  **Mechanical Restraint:** The use of a device to prevent, restrict, or subdue a person's movement for the primary purpose of influencing a person's behaviour but does not include the use of devices for therapeutic or non-behavioural purposes.
4.  **Physical Restraint:** The use or action of physical force to prevent, restrict, or subdue movement of a person's body, or part of their body, for the primary purpose of influencing their behaviour. Physical restraint does not include the use of a hands-on technique in a reflexive way to guide or redirect a person away from potential harm/injury, consistent with what could reasonably be considered the exercise of care towards a person.
5.  **Environmental Restraint:** Restricts a person's free access to all parts of their environment, including items or activities.

### 2.2 Authorisation and Reporting Requirements

*   **Authorisation:** Restrictive practices must be authorised by the relevant state or territory body (e.g., the Victorian Senior Practitioner) before they can be legally implemented.
*   **Behaviour Support Plan (BSP):** Any use of a regulated restrictive practice must be documented in a comprehensive, evidence-based BSP developed by an NDIS Behaviour Support Practitioner. The plan must include strategies to reduce and eventually eliminate the need for the restrictive practice.
*   **Monthly Reporting:** Providers implementing restrictive practices must submit monthly reports to the NDIS Commission detailing the frequency and duration of each use, even if the practice is authorised and included in a BSP.
*   **Unauthorised Use:** Any use of a restrictive practice that is not authorised, or not in accordance with a BSP, is a reportable incident and must be notified to the NDIS Commission within the timeframes outlined in Section 1.2.

### 2.3 Behaviour Support Plan Documentation

A compliant Behaviour Support Plan must include:
*   A functional behaviour assessment identifying the purpose of the behaviour of concern.
*   Proactive strategies to address the underlying causes of the behaviour and improve the participant's quality of life.
*   Reactive strategies to safely manage the behaviour when it occurs.
*   Clear protocols for the use of any restrictive practices, including specific triggers, duration limits, and monitoring requirements.
*   A plan for fading out the use of restrictive practices over time.

## 3. State-Specific Requirements: Victoria

In Victoria, the authorisation of restrictive practices is governed by Part 6A of the *Disability Act 2006* (Vic).

### 3.1 The Victorian Senior Practitioner

The Victorian Senior Practitioner is responsible for protecting the rights of persons with disability who are subject to restrictive interventions and compulsory treatment. They oversee the authorisation process and monitor the use of these practices.

### 3.2 Authorised Program Officers (APOs)

Disability service providers and registered NDIS providers in Victoria must appoint an Authorised Program Officer (APO). The APO is responsible for:
*   Approving the inclusion of restrictive practices in a participant's Behaviour Support Plan.
*   Ensuring that the use of restrictive practices complies with the *Disability Act 2006* and the NDIS Rules.
*   Reporting the use of restrictive practices to the Victorian Senior Practitioner via the Restrictive Intervention Data System (RIDS).

### 3.3 Authorisation Process and Timelines

*   **Independent Person:** Before an APO can approve a restrictive practice, an Independent Person must be involved to explain the proposed practice to the participant and ascertain their views.
*   **Interim Behaviour Support Plans:** The Victorian Senior Practitioner authorises the use of restrictive practices in an Interim BSP for a maximum period of 6 months.
*   **Comprehensive Behaviour Support Plans:** The authorisation for restrictive practices in a Comprehensive BSP is valid for a maximum of 12 months.
*   **RIDS Reporting:** Providers must use the Restrictive Intervention Data System (RIDS) to submit BSPs for authorisation and to report on the ongoing use of restrictive practices.

### 3.4 Compulsory Reporting

In addition to NDIS Commission reporting, Victorian providers must comply with state-based compulsory reporting requirements, such as reporting suspected child abuse to Child Protection under the *Children, Youth and Families Act 2005* (Vic).
