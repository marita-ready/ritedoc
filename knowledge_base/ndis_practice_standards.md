# NDIS Practice Standards and Legislative Framework

This document provides an exhaustive, production-ready reference for the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) Practice Standards, the NDIS Code of Conduct, key provisions of the NDIS Act 2013, and the significant legislative changes taking effect through to July 2026. It serves as the foundational knowledge base for RiteDoc's compliance engine.

## Part 1: NDIS Practice Standards (Core Module)

The NDIS Practice Standards specify the quality standards that registered NDIS providers must meet to provide supports and services to NDIS participants. The Core Module applies to all registered providers and encompasses four key outcome areas.

### 1. Rights and Responsibilities

This outcome area ensures that participants' rights are upheld and respected during service delivery.

*   **Person-Centred Supports:** Each participant accesses supports that promote, uphold, and respect their legal and human rights. Providers must ensure that supports are delivered in a way that enables participants to exercise choice and control.
*   **Individual Values and Beliefs:** Each participant accesses supports that respect their culture, diversity, values, and beliefs. This includes respecting the cultural and linguistic diversity of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples and people from culturally and linguistically diverse backgrounds.
*   **Privacy and Dignity:** Each participant accesses supports that respect and protect their dignity and right to privacy. Information must be managed securely, and personal care must be provided in a dignified manner.
*   **Independence and Informed Choice:** Each participant is supported by the provider to make informed choices, exercise control, and maximise their independence relating to the supports provided.
*   **Violence, Abuse, Neglect, Exploitation, and Discrimination:** Each participant accesses supports free from violence, abuse, neglect, exploitation, or discrimination. Providers must have robust systems to prevent, identify, and respond to any such incidents.

### 2. Provider Governance and Operational Management

This area focuses on the systems and processes required to manage a compliant and effective organisation.

*   **Governance and Operational Management:** The provider's governing body ensures that the organisation is managed effectively and meets its obligations under the NDIS Act 2013 and relevant rules.
*   **Risk Management:** Risks to participants, workers, and the provider are identified and managed. This includes clinical, operational, and financial risks.
*   **Quality Management:** The provider maintains a quality management system relevant and proportionate to the size and scale of the provider and the scope and complexity of the supports delivered.
*   **Information Management:** Each participant's information is managed to ensure that it is identifiable, accurately recorded, current, and confidential.
*   **Feedback and Complaints Management:** Each participant has knowledge of and access to the provider's complaints management and resolution system. Complaints made by all parties are welcomed, acknowledged, respected, and well-managed.
*   **Incident Management:** Each participant is safeguarded by the provider's incident management system, ensuring that incidents are acknowledged, responded to, well-managed, and learned from.
*   **Human Resource Management:** Each participant's support needs are met by workers who are competent in relation to their role, hold relevant qualifications, and who have relevant expertise and experience to provide person-centred support.
*   **Continuity of Supports:** Each participant accesses timely and appropriate support without interruption.

### 3. Provision of Supports

This area details the requirements for planning and delivering supports to participants.

*   **Access to Supports:** Each participant accesses the most appropriate supports that meet their needs, goals, and preferences.
*   **Support Planning:** Each participant is actively involved in the development of their support plans. Support plans reflect participant needs, requirements, preferences, strengths, and goals, and are regularly reviewed.
*   **Service Agreements with Participants:** Each participant has a clear understanding of the supports they have chosen and how they will be provided.
*   **Responsive Support Provision:** Each participant accesses responsive, timely, competent, and appropriate supports to meet their needs, desired outcomes, and goals.
*   **Transitions to or from the Provider:** Each participant experiences a planned and coordinated transition to or from the provider.
*   **Mealtime Management:** (Where applicable) Each participant requiring mealtime management receives meals, snacks, and drinks that are nutritious, of a texture that is appropriate to their individual needs, and delivered in a way that is safe and enjoyable.

### 4. Support Provision Environment

This area covers the physical environment where supports are delivered.

*   **Safe Environment:** Each participant accesses supports in a safe environment that is appropriate to their needs.
*   **Participant Money and Property:** Participant money and property is secure, and each participant can use their own money and property as they determine.
*   **Management of Medication:** Each participant requiring medication is confident their provider administers, stores, and monitors the effects of their medication and works to prevent errors or incidents.
*   **Waste Management:** Each participant, each worker, and any other person in the home is protected from harm as a result of exposure to waste, infectious, or hazardous substances generated during the delivery of supports.
*   **Emergency and Disaster Management:** Emergency and disaster management includes planning that ensures that the risks to the health, safety, and wellbeing of participants that may arise in an emergency or disaster are considered and mitigated.

## Part 2: Supplementary Modules

Providers delivering specific, higher-risk supports must comply with relevant supplementary modules.

### High Intensity Daily Personal Activities

This module applies to providers supporting participants who rely on high-intensity daily personal activities. Workers must have the skills and knowledge described in the high-intensity support skills descriptors.

The eight specific high-intensity supports are:
1.  **Complex Bowel Care:** Requires participant involvement in planning, regular health practitioner review, and specific worker training.
2.  **Enteral Feeding and Management:** Requires assessment, planning, and worker training specific to the type and method of enteral feeding.
3.  **Severe Dysphagia Management:** Requires assessment by a qualified health practitioner, a detailed management plan, and specific worker training delivered by a health practitioner.
4.  **Tracheostomy Management:** Requires a detailed plan for suctioning and management, including emergency response protocols.
5.  **Urinary Catheter Management:** Requires appropriate planning and worker training for catheter care.
6.  **Ventilator Management:** Requires comprehensive planning, risk management, and specific worker training for ventilator support.
7.  **Subcutaneous Injections:** Requires specific training and protocols for the safe administration of subcutaneous injections.
8.  **Complex Wound Management:** Requires assessment and planning by a qualified health practitioner and specific worker training.

### Specialist Behaviour Support

This module applies to providers registered to provide specialist behaviour support, including the development of behaviour support plans.

*   **Behaviour Support in the NDIS:** Participants access behaviour support that incorporates evidence-informed practice and complies with relevant legislation.
*   **Restrictive Practices:** Participants are only subject to restrictive practices that meet state/territory authorisation requirements and Commonwealth legislation. Alternatives must be promoted, and plans must include strategies to reduce and eliminate restrictive practices.
*   **Functional Behaviour Assessments and Behaviour Support Plans:** Quality of life is improved by tailored, evidence-informed plans based on functional behaviour assessments.
*   **Supporting the Implementation of the Behaviour Support Plan:** Plans are implemented effectively through training, coaching, and mentoring of the providers implementing the plan.

### Early Childhood Supports

This module applies to providers delivering early childhood supports.

*   **The Child:** Supports promote the child's rights, functional skills, and meaningful participation.
*   **The Family:** Families receive family-centred, culturally inclusive supports that focus on their strengths.
*   **Inclusion:** Supports engage natural environments and enable inclusive participation in family and community life.
*   **Collaboration:** Coordinated supports from a collaborative team (family, provider, other professionals).
*   **Capacity Building:** Supports build the knowledge and skills of the family and collaborating providers.
*   **Evidence-Informed Practice:** Supports are based on validated practices and best available research.
*   **Outcome-Based Approach:** Supports are goal-focused, with functional outcomes measured and evaluated.

### Specialist Disability Accommodation (SDA)

This module applies to providers of Specialist Disability Accommodation.

*   **Rights and Responsibilities:** Access to SDA is consistent with legal and human rights, supporting informed choice and control.
*   **Conflict of Interest:** The right to exercise choice over other NDIS supports is not limited by the choice of SDA dwelling.
*   **Service Agreements with Participants:** Participants understand the terms and conditions of their SDA and tenancy agreements.
*   **Enrolment of SDA Properties:** Dwellings meet the requirements of their design type and category.
*   **Tenancy Management:** Participants are supported by effective tenancy management that adheres to relevant rules and legislation.

### Implementing Behaviour Support Plans

This module applies to providers who implement behaviour support plans (but do not necessarily develop them).
*   **Behaviour Support in the NDIS:** Providers must understand and apply evidence-informed behaviour support practices.
*   **Restrictive Practices:** Providers must only use restrictive practices that are authorised and documented in a Behaviour Support Plan.
*   **Supporting the Implementation of the Behaviour Support Plan:** Workers must be trained and supported to implement the plan effectively.

### Specialist Support Coordination

This module applies to providers delivering specialist support coordination.
*   **Specialist Support Coordination:** Participants receive specialist support coordination that is person-centred, builds capacity, and addresses complex barriers.
*   **Management of a Participant's NDIS Supports:** Participants are supported to understand and implement their NDIS plan, negotiate service agreements, and manage their budget.
*   **Conflict of Interest:** Providers must manage conflicts of interest, particularly when providing both support coordination and other funded supports.

### Verification Module

This module applies to providers delivering lower-risk supports and services. It requires a lighter-touch audit process compared to the Certification (Core) Module.
*   **Human Resource Management:** Workers have the necessary qualifications, skills, and experience.
*   **Incident Management:** The provider has a system to identify, manage, and resolve incidents.
*   **Complaints Management:** The provider has a system to receive and resolve complaints.
*   **Risk Management:** The provider identifies and manages risks to participants, workers, and the organisation.

## Part 3: NDIS Code of Conduct

The NDIS Code of Conduct applies to all NDIS providers (registered and unregistered) and all persons employed or otherwise engaged by an NDIS provider. It comprises eight fundamental obligations:

1.  Act with respect for individual rights to freedom of expression, self-determination, and decision-making in accordance with relevant laws and conventions.
2.  Respect the privacy of people with disability.
3.  Provide supports and services in a safe and competent manner with care and skill.
4.  Act with integrity, honesty, and transparency.
5.  Promptly take steps to raise and act on concerns about matters that might have an impact on the quality and safety of supports provided to people with disability.
6.  Take all reasonable steps to prevent and respond to all forms of violence, exploitation, neglect, and abuse of people with disability.
7.  Take all reasonable steps to prevent and respond to sexual misconduct.
8.  **Fair Pricing:** Not charge a higher price for goods or services simply because the person is an NDIS participant (this replaces the previous obligations 8 and 9 regarding unlawful/unethical and corrupt/fraudulent practices, which are now covered under broader fraud legislation).

## Part 4: NDIS Act 2013 Key Provisions

The *National Disability Insurance Scheme Act 2013* (NDIS Act) establishes the NDIS and the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission. Key provisions relevant to documentation and compliance include:

*   **Section 10 (NDIS Supports):** Defines what constitutes an NDIS support that can be funded by the scheme.
*   **Section 21 (Separate Pathways):** Outlines the pathways for accessing the NDIS based on disability requirements or early intervention requirements.
*   **Section 24 (Disability Requirements):** Specifies the criteria a person must meet regarding their disability to access the scheme.
*   **Section 25 (Early Intervention Requirements):** Specifies the criteria for accessing the scheme under early intervention.
*   **Section 33 (Funding Periods):** Establishes the framework for total funding amounts, funding component amounts, and funding periods within a participant's plan.
*   **Section 34 (Reasonable and Necessary Supports):** Mandates that the NDIS will only fund supports that are reasonable and necessary and relate to the impairments that meet the access requirements.

## Part 5: July 2026 Legislative Changes and Reforms

Significant reforms are being implemented to strengthen the integrity and safety of the NDIS, culminating in major changes taking effect in July 2026.

### Getting the NDIS Back on Track Act 2024

This Act introduced foundational changes that pave the way for future reforms:
*   **Clarified NDIS Supports:** Stricter definitions of what the NDIS will fund, distinguishing NDIS responsibilities from mainstream systems.
*   **Impairment Notices:** Participants receive formal notices listing the specific impairments for which they met access requirements, ensuring funding is directly linked to these impairments.
*   **Funding Periods:** Transitioning to structured funding periods (e.g., 3-month periods starting May 2025) to help participants manage their budgets.

### NDIS Amendment (Integrity and Safeguarding) Bill 2025

Introduced in late 2025 and passed in early 2026, this Bill significantly strengthens the regulatory powers of the NDIS Commission:
*   **Increased Penalties:** Maximum civil penalties for harming a participant are increased to align with Work Health and Safety legislation.
*   **Expanded Banning Orders:** The Commission can now ban bad actors from operating as NDIS auditors, business advisors, and consultants, not just direct support providers.
*   **Enhanced Information Sharing:** Improved mechanisms for the NDIA and the NDIS Commission to share information regarding provider conduct and participant safety.
*   **Stricter Registration Requirements:** Tighter criteria for becoming and remaining a registered provider, focusing on the suitability of key personnel.

### Mandatory Registration (Effective 1 July 2026)

A critical reform taking effect on 1 July 2026 is the introduction of mandatory registration for specific provider types:
*   **Supported Independent Living (SIL) Providers:** All SIL providers must be registered with the NDIS Commission.
*   **Platform Providers:** Providers operating digital platforms connecting participants with workers must also be registered.
*   **New SIL Practice Standards:** The Commission is developing and testing new Practice Standards specifically for SIL, focusing on quality and safety in shared accommodation and improving guidance for frontline workers. These new standards will be enforceable from 1 July 2026.

## Part 6: Worker Screening Requirements

The NDIS Worker Screening Check is a mandatory requirement for all workers of registered NDIS providers engaged in risk-assessed roles.

*   **Risk-Assessed Roles:** Includes roles involving direct delivery of specified supports, roles with more than incidental contact with participants, and key personnel roles.
*   **Clearance Validity:** A clearance is valid for five years across all states and territories.
*   **Ongoing Monitoring:** Workers with a clearance are subject to ongoing monitoring. If a worker commits a relevant offence, their clearance can be revoked.
*   **Unregistered Providers:** While not mandatory for unregistered providers, participants who self-manage or use a plan manager can request that their unregistered providers ensure their workers have an NDIS Worker Screening clearance.

## Part 7: Pricing Arrangements and Price Limits

The NDIA publishes the NDIS Pricing Arrangements and Price Limits, which set the maximum prices registered providers can charge for specific supports.

*   **Documentation Requirements:** Providers must maintain comprehensive documentation (such as progress notes) to substantiate claims for payment. The documentation must clearly demonstrate that the billed hours match the delivered support and that the support aligns with the participant's plan goals.
*   **Audit Scrutiny:** Auditors heavily scrutinise progress notes to verify service delivery against billed hours. Vague or incomplete notes that do not detail the specific support provided or its alignment with goals are a major compliance risk and can lead to funding recovery.

## Part 8: Specific Documentation Requirements

To ensure compliance and mitigate audit risks, providers must adhere to strict documentation standards across several key areas.

### 8.1 Pricing and Billing Documentation

*   **Exact Shift Times:** Progress notes must record the exact start and end times of the shift, not just the total duration.
*   **Travel Documentation:** Claims for provider travel must be supported by documentation detailing the travel time, distance, and the specific participant the travel was for.
*   **Cancellation Documentation:** If a participant cancels a shift, the provider must document the time of the cancellation, the reason (if known), and whether it meets the criteria for a short-notice cancellation.
*   **Short Notice Cancellations:** Providers can only claim for a short-notice cancellation if the participant gives less than 7 clear days' notice (or as specified in the current Pricing Arrangements) and the provider is unable to find alternative billable work for the worker. This must be clearly documented.

### 8.2 Medication Management

*   **PRN Medication:** The administration of PRN (as-needed) medication must be meticulously documented, including the time, dosage, the specific reason/symptom prompting the administration, and the participant's response/outcome after administration.
*   **Controlled Substances:** The storage, administration, and disposal of controlled substances (e.g., Schedule 8 drugs) must follow strict protocols, including double-signing by authorised staff and regular stock counts, with all actions recorded in a dedicated register.

### 8.3 Complaints Management

*   **Documentation:** All complaints, whether formal or informal, must be documented in the provider's complaints register.
*   **Details Required:** The record must include the date of the complaint, the nature of the issue, the actions taken to investigate and resolve it, and the communication with the complainant regarding the outcome.

### 8.4 Consent and Capacity

*   **Informed Consent:** Providers must document that informed consent was obtained before delivering supports, sharing information, or implementing significant changes to a support plan.
*   **Capacity Assessments:** If there are concerns about a participant's capacity to make a specific decision, the provider must document the steps taken to support decision-making or the involvement of a legally appointed substitute decision-maker (e.g., a guardian or administrator).
