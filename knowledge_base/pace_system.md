# NDIS PACE System: Comprehensive Guide for Providers

This document provides a comprehensive overview of the NDIS PACE system, detailing its functionality, rollout, and the critical changes it introduces for providers regarding documentation, claiming, and compliance. It serves as a core reference for the RiteDoc compliance engine.

## 1. What is PACE?

PACE is the new computer system implemented by the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA) to replace the legacy SAP CRM system and the original myplace portals [1]. The system is designed to improve the participant experience, enhance plan security, and streamline interactions between participants, providers, and the NDIA.

The transition to PACE involves the rollout of a new "my NDIS" participant portal and a new "my NDIS" provider portal. However, for financial transactions, providers continue to use the existing myplace provider portal [2].

### 1.1 Rollout and Transition

The PACE system was introduced nationally in October 2023 [3]. The transition is gradual:
*   All new NDIS participants have their plans built in the PACE system.
*   Existing participants are progressively transitioned to PACE when their current plans undergo reassessment or renewal.
*   During the transition period, providers must operate across both the legacy system and the PACE system, depending on which system a specific participant's plan is built in.

## 2. Key Changes Introduced by PACE

PACE introduces several fundamental changes to how NDIS plans are structured, managed, and claimed against.

### 2.1 Removal of Service Bookings

One of the most significant changes in PACE is the complete removal of service bookings [2]. In the legacy system, service bookings were used to allocate specific funds to a provider for a defined period. The NDIA removed service bookings to give participants greater flexibility and choice in how they use their funding.

While this increases participant control, it removes the funding surety that service bookings previously provided to providers. Providers must now rely on robust service agreements and the new "my providers" system to secure payment.

### 2.2 Support Categories and Budget Flexibility

PACE restructures how funding is categorised and managed within a participant's plan.

*   **New Support Categories:** PACE introduces new support categories, bringing the total to 21 (up from 15 in the legacy system) [2].
*   **Budget Level:** Budgets are now lifted to the support category level, rather than being restricted to specific line items [2].
*   **Support Types:** PACE organises supports into broader "support types" (e.g., Daily living, Social and community participation, Home and living supports) [4].
*   **Flexibility:** Participants generally have more flexibility to move funding between support types within the same broader category (e.g., within Core Supports) [4].
*   **Stated Supports:** While Core supports are generally flexible, Capacity Building and Capital supports may still have "stated" support categories, meaning funds can only be used for the specific purpose outlined [3].

### 2.3 The "My Providers" System

To replace the security previously offered by service bookings, PACE introduces the "my providers" system [5]. This allows participants to formally record their preferred, long-term providers on their NDIS plan.

*   **Purpose:** Recording a provider as a "my provider" signals to the NDIA that the participant has an established relationship with that provider and authorises them to receive payments from the participant's plan [5].
*   **Payment Speed:** Claims submitted by a recorded "my provider" are generally processed and paid faster (typically within 2 to 3 days) [5].
*   **Non-Recorded Providers:** Participants can still use providers who are not recorded as a "my provider". However, when an unrecorded provider submits a claim, the NDIA initiates an additional validation process. The participant (or their nominee) receives an SMS to confirm they agreed to the support. This validation process delays payment, generally taking up to 10 days [5].

#### Mandatory Endorsement

For certain high-risk or specialised supports, being recorded as a "my provider" is **mandatory**, regardless of how the participant's plan is managed (Agency-managed, plan-managed, or self-managed) [5]. Claims for these supports will be automatically rejected if the provider is not recorded. These mandatory supports include:
*   Specialist Disability Accommodation (SDA)
*   Home and Living Supports (including Supported Independent Living - SIL)
*   Behaviour Supports (including the use of restrictive practices)

### 2.4 Funding Periods

To help participants manage their budgets and reduce the risk of overspending, the NDIA has introduced structured funding periods [6].

*   **Implementation:** While introduced in legislation in October 2024, the active enforcement of shorter funding periods (e.g., 3-month periods) began rolling out gradually from May 2025 for new and reassessed plans [6].
*   **Functionality:** A funding period dictates when a specific portion of a participant's total budget becomes available for use. It does not change the total annual funding amount [6].
*   **Claiming Rules:** Providers can only claim for services delivered *within* the active funding period. Claiming for services delivered before the funds are released, or attempting to claim against a future funding period, will result in claim rejection [4]. Unused funds typically roll over to the next period [4].

## 3. Documentation and Claiming Requirements

While the underlying system has changed, the fundamental requirements for robust documentation remain critical, and in some areas, have become more stringent due to enhanced system controls.

### 3.1 Claim Submission

*   **Portal:** Providers continue to use the legacy myplace provider portal to submit claims for payment [2].
*   **Codes:** There are no changes to the NDIS support item descriptions or the codes used to make claims. The existing codes automatically map to the relevant budgets in the PACE system [2].
*   **Overnight Payments:** The legacy system's overnight fund release process no longer occurs. All claims are subject to a minimum 24-hour processing time, with complex claims or those requiring validation taking up to 10 days [3].

### 3.2 Critical Documentation Implications

The introduction of PACE and its associated controls necessitates precise documentation in progress notes and invoices.

1.  **Exact Dates of Service:** Because of the strict enforcement of funding periods, progress notes and invoices must clearly and accurately document the exact date the service was delivered. The date of service must fall within an active funding period with available funds [6].
2.  **Alignment with Support Types:** Documentation must clearly describe the support provided in a way that aligns with the broader PACE support types (e.g., Daily living, Social and community participation) to justify the claim against the relevant category [4].
3.  **Evidence of Agreement:** With the removal of service bookings, the service agreement and the progress notes serve as the primary evidence that the participant agreed to the support. Notes should consistently reflect the participant's engagement and consent.

### 3.3 Support Coordinator Reporting

PACE introduces standardised reporting templates specifically for Support Coordinators and Psychosocial Recovery Coaches [7].

*   **Templates:** The NDIA has provided specific forms (Progress Report and Implementation Report) that must be used [7].
*   **Content:** These templates require detailed information on the participant's support needs, the supports they are receiving, and their progress toward implementing their plan and achieving their goals [7].
*   **Submission:** Reports must be submitted as attachments through the new my NDIS provider portal (not the myplace portal) [7].

## 4. Interaction with 2026 Reforms

The PACE system provides the technological infrastructure necessary to enforce the legislative reforms taking effect through to July 2026.

*   **Impairment Notices:** PACE's improved business intelligence allows the NDIA to better track spending. This aligns with the 2024 reforms requiring funding to be directly linked to the specific impairments listed in a participant's formal Impairment Notice.
*   **Enhanced Scrutiny:** The system controls in PACE, including the "my providers" validation and the monitoring of spending patterns, support the NDIS Commission's expanded regulatory powers to detect and prevent fraud, overbilling, and non-compliant practices.
