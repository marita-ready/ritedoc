## 4. Group Engagement Tips

If you just drop a link in a Facebook group, it will likely be ignored or deleted by admins. Here is how to build trust and get people interested in RiteDoc without being spammy.

**Step 1: Introduce Yourself First**
When you join a group, make a simple introduction post. 
*Example:* "Hi everyone! I'm Marita. I've been working in the NDIS compliance space for a while. Just joined to connect with other providers and support workers and learn about the challenges you're facing day-to-day. Looking forward to chatting!"

**Step 2: Be Helpful (The Give, Give, Ask Method)**
Spend your first week just answering questions. If someone asks about compliance, audits, or managing burnout, jump in with genuine advice. Do not mention RiteDoc yet. Build your credibility as an expert.

**Step 3: Ask a "Pain Point" Question**
Before pitching, start a conversation about the problem you solve. 
*Example:* "Hey support workers, quick question—how much time do you spend writing progress notes after your shifts? I've been hearing from a lot of people that it's causing major burnout. How do you manage it?"
When people reply, validate their struggles. 

**Step 4: The Soft Pitch**
Once you have engaged with people, you can make a post about RiteDoc, but frame it as a story rather than an ad.
*Example:* "A few weeks ago, I asked how much time you all spend on progress notes, and the answers were exhausting! That's actually why I built RiteDoc. It's an offline app that rewrites your messy notes into audit-ready drafts. I'm looking for 17 founders to test it out. Let me know if you want the link!" (Wait for them to comment "Yes", then reply to their comment with the link).
